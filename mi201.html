Analysis of Strava's Social Media Presence

Strava is a social media company made specifically for athletes. The social network is primarily focused on their mobile app so users can take advantage of the GPS aspect of the app to keep track of their workout and then share with their users. The company allows for users to track their workouts and to build a community around their athletic accomplishments. As Strava is a social network company, they still need to advertise and build a strong user base for their network and they do this by using a combination of strategies. Strava has their own blog, they post YouTube videos to their channel and they interact with users on Twitter and Facebook.

Strava's social media profiles includes 2 Facebook accounts based on the main activity of the user (Running/Cycling), Twitter and Youtube. Strava has an Instagram but it is not promoted through their official website. Their website includes a blog that contains fresh material meant to engage readers by pertaining to their interest in health and fitness. By interacting more with users on different social media, they help broaden their audience and be aware of what users are looking for when they look at Strava for content.

Strava tends to be active on all of their social media fronts but they especially tend to give praise through these accounts to their users for being active and for using Strava to track their workouts. These types of posts tend to showcase how users tend to use the social network and how versatile the app can be. Users can show off their workouts on other social media or mention Strava in a tweet, like the tweet below does.

Since Strava's social network is used mainly through their mobile app, they focused on promoting their app more than their website and they tried to appeal to both Android and iPhone users. Through the app, users can track their activity or sync it with another fitness device and people will be able to comment or give you kudos for each workout. Users are able to feature Strava and mention the brand on Instagram based on their enjoyability of the application.

Several blogs mention using Strava to track their progress and to be competitive through an app. This blog post talks about how the featured blogger's friends use Strava for competitive races in order to increase motivation and how one friend was using it to display her cycling progress to fundraise for a charity. The 2nd blog post interviews a pro cyclist and talks about how he trains. He discussed how he used Strava to compare his times with others and keep track of his goals. With their app being so accessible and usable, it makes it easier for bloggers and users to mention the app without worrying others will not be able to understand what it is.

Strava's Instragram page makes it easier for athletes to post their workout and interact with their company. People are able to engage with Strava and show others how well their workout went. Strava is able to have their users market their product out and also attract more people who tend to share the same interests as Strava's users or Strava.
